# 熱鬧 — encoding-layer page

> A memory-encoding page (PRD §5.5). Deeper than the dictionary entry: etymology, mnemonics, associations, and *why it's tricky*. Opens when you click the word in SRS review.

## Etymology / character story
- **熱** (rè) — *hot*. 灬 (fire, the four-dot "fire" radical) at the bottom. Heat.
- **鬧** (nào) — *noisy, to make a racket*. 鬥 "fight/quarrel" wrapping 市 "market" → a noisy, jostling marketplace.
- Together: **heat + market-noise = a buzzing, bustling, lively scene.**

## Mnemonic
A **hot** (熱) **market** crammed with people all **shouting** (鬧) at once — that's 熱鬧. Picture the 夜市 you just read about: steam off the grills, vendors yelling, a happy crush of people.

## Semantic associations
- Opposite: **安靜** (ān jìng) "quiet, calm."
- Family by sound/shape: **鬧鐘** (nào zhōng) "alarm clock" — literally a "noise-bell." (This is the confusion you flagged.)

## Why it's tricky (your flag)
You keep colliding 熱**鬧** with **鬧**鐘. Anchor: 鬧 alone = *noise*. 熱鬧 = *good, festive noise* (a party, a market). 鬧鐘 = *annoying noise on purpose* (an alarm waking you up). Same 鬧, opposite feelings.

## Vivid example
過年的時候，街上又**熱鬧**又吵，但是大家都很開心。
